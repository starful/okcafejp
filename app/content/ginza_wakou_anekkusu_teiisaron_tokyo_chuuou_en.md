## Main Feature Analysis

### The Heritage of Wako and the Birth of the Annex
To truly appreciate the magic of 銀座・和光アネックス ティーサロン, one must first understand the heritage of its parent establishment. Wako (和光) was founded in 1947, though its roots trace back to the K. Hattori watch shop established in 1881 by Kintaro Hattori, the founder of Seiko. The main Wako building, with its distinctive neo-Renaissance architecture and grand curved facade, has stood as the symbolic heart of Ginza for generations. It represents quality, luxury, and impeccable taste.

As the decades progressed, Wako expanded its vision to encompass the finer things in life beyond timepieces and jewelry. This expansion culminated in the creation of the Wako Annex (和光アネックス), a dedicated building positioned just behind the main department store. The Annex was designed specifically to showcase the brand's culinary artistry, featuring a boutique cake shop, a chocolate salon, and, of course, the celebrated Tea Salon on the second floor. By separating the dining experience into its own dedicated space, Wako succeeded in creating an exclusive micro-universe of culinary indulgence, away from the retail floors.

### Culinary Artistry: The Seasonal Parfait and Beyond
At the heart of the salon's widespread acclaim is its uncompromising approach to ingredients and presentation. While the cafe offers a wide array of classic European and Japanese-inspired sweets, it is perhaps most famous for its seasonal parfaits (季節のパフェ). In Japan, the appreciation of changing seasons is deeply embedded in the culinary culture, and the patissiers at Wako Annex elevate this concept to an art form.

Depending on the time of year you visit, the menu transforms to highlight the finest seasonal fruits at their peak of sweetness and flavor. During the spring, patrons are treated to delicate strawberry parfaits that layer fresh, hand-selected berries with artisanal strawberry sorbet, rich vanilla bean cream, and delicate crumbles for texture. As summer arrives, the menu shifts to showcase juicy melons, luscious peaches, or tropical mangoes. Autumn brings the earthy richness of chestnuts—often transformed into a sophisticated Mont Blanc parfait—and sweet potatoes, while winter highlights deep, complex chocolate creations and citrus fruits. 

Every component of these parfaits is meticulously crafted in-house. Rather than relying on heavy syrups or artificial colorings, the chefs focus on balancing textures and temperatures. A single spoonful of a Wako parfait might contain a contrast of warm and cold, smooth and crunchy, sweet and tart, ensuring that every bite is dynamic and engaging.

A world-class tea salon requires an equally impressive beverage selection, and 銀座・和光アネックス ティーサロン does not disappoint. The tea menu is a curated journey through the finest tea-producing regions of the world. From premium single-origin black teas like Darjeeling and Assam to delicate green teas and aromatic herbal infusions, every selection is chosen to complement the pastry menu.

The preparation of the tea is treated with the same level of precision as the desserts. Water temperature, steeping time, and the choice of teaware are carefully calibrated for each specific variety. The tea is typically served in elegant, heavy porcelain teapots alongside matching cups, allowing the tea to retain its optimal temperature. Guests are encouraged to savor the aroma of the leaves before taking their first sip, turning the simple act of drinking tea into a mindful, meditative ritual. For those who prefer coffee, the salon also offers beautifully brewed siphon or drip coffees, characterized by smooth finishes and rich aromas.
